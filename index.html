    Physics Master's Thesis Researcher

    Leiden University

    Aug 2018 - Dec 2018

    • Prepared a setup to achieve high precision real-time molecular characterization by analyzing tunneling current through molecules using edges of two twisted graphene layers as the electrodes in Scanning Tunneling Microscope (STM).

    • Replaced STM tip and base with custom-made clamps. Etched single layer graphene to prepare electrodes of one atom precision in clean room.

  • Thesis abstract: The study of electron transport in single-molecule experiments can result in a breakthrough in the research on the characterization of the molecules, if we can exploit the nature of tunneling current flowing through them. Graphene, being a sheet of one-atom thickness, might offer the best solution to the current problem of the development of the proper architecture of electrodes to achieve high precision results. Using edges of two twisted graphene layers as the electrodes, we aim to analyze the tunneling current through nanoscale gaps and single molecules. As a substance translocates between the electrodes, its structural differences are reflected as fluctuations in the tunneling current. We are looking out for patterns in these fluctuations. This study will serve a higher purpose of understanding and developing molecular electronics, bio-sensing, and a method for DNA sequencing.

    What factors contributed to choosing such an unconventional career trajectory?

    My academic journey, combining M.Sc. Physics with B.E. Manufacturing Engineering, was propelled by an indefinite passion for physics and CAD/CAM interest. During the final year of this education, I conducted experimental physics research at Leiden University, implementing a PID control system for STM. This experience unveiled the allure of coding, translating all efforts into tangible output, unlike experimental work (which is fun but) with bottlenecks like repair work and delivery of apparatus/chemicals.

    In physics, problems are tackled by intelligently guessing a hypothesis and later verifying it experimentally using observed data. In my view, AI is just an improved version of it where hypothesis is derived from data which after is verified on data. Recognizing this parallel between AI and physics, I pivoted towards AI, as the manufacturing industry in India lacked advanced technology adoption.
