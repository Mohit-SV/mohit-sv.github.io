What factors contributed to choosing such an unconventional career trajectory?

My academic journey, combining M.Sc. Physics with B.E. Manufacturing Engineering, was propelled by an indefinite passion for physics and CAD/CAM interest. During the final year of this education, I conducted experimental physics research at Leiden University, implementing a PID control system for STM. This experience unveiled the allure of coding, translating all efforts into tangible output, unlike experimental work (which is fun but) with bottlenecks like repair work and delivery of apparatus/chemicals.

In physics, problems are tackled by intelligently guessing a hypothesis and later verifying it experimentally using observed data. In my view, AI is just an improved version of it where hypothesis is derived from data which after is verified on data. Recognizing this parallel between AI and physics, I pivoted towards AI, as the manufacturing industry in India lacked advanced technology adoption.

I began with a backend-focused internship, followed by a full-time backend-AI role. To dive deeper into AI, I left the full-time job and started applying for M.Sc. AI.
